About Us

Welcome to Logos Bookstore! We are a team of avid readers and book enthusiasts who are dedicated to fostering a love for literature and learning. Our carefully curated collection of books spans a wide range of genres, from fiction to non-fiction, history to science, and beyond. As a locally owned and operated bookstore, we are proud to be a part of our community, and we strive to create a welcoming space where book lovers can gather, explore, and connect. Our knowledgeable staff is always ready to provide personalized recommendations and engage in meaningful conversations about books and ideas. We are committed to promoting literacy, supporting local authors, and creating a haven for all bookworms to indulge in their passion for reading. Thank you for choosing Logos Bookstore, and we can't wait to share the joy of reading with you!

Our values

People

We value our community by fostering a culture of inclusivity and diversity, where everyone is welcomed and respected. They also prioritize employee development and empowerment, ensuring that staff have the tools and resources to succeed and make meaningful contributions to the bookstore's mission.

Product

We value our products by offering a wide variety of books that cater to diverse interests and perspectives, and by ensuring that every book they sell meets high standards of quality and content. They also prioritize sustainability by sourcing environmentally-friendly products and materials, and by supporting local publishers and authors.

Planet

Our values of a planet are centered around sustainability, biodiversity, and preservation of natural resources. They prioritize the long-term health and well-being of the planet and its inhabitants, and aim to promote ecological balance and harmony with nature.

Random facts about our store

  • Logos was founded in 1923 by a group of passionate book collectors who wanted to share their love of literature with the world.
  • The original Logos store was located in a small town in rural America, and was known for its collection of rare and out-of-print books.
  • In the 1960s, Logos moved to a larger location in the city, and began to focus on offering a wider variety of books to cater to different tastes and interests.
  • Logos was one of the first bookstores to offer an online ordering and shipping service in the 1990s, which helped to expand its reach and customer base.
  • The current owner of Logos, a young woman named Emma, inherited the store from her grandfather and has worked to modernize and update the business while still maintaining its traditional values.
  • Logos has a dedicated children's section with a colorful mural and a puppet theater for storytelling and events.
  • The bookstore hosts an annual literary festival, which attracts authors, readers, and book lovers from all over the world.
  • Logos has a community outreach program that partners with local schools and libraries to promote literacy and reading.
  • The bookstore has an in-house bookbinding and restoration service, which specializes in restoring rare and antique books to their original condition.
  • Logos has a sustainability initiative that encourages customers to bring in their old books for recycling, and uses eco-friendly materials and packaging for its products.
